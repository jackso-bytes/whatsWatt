About these cost estimates

Generation cost data is taken from the UK Government's Electricity Generation Costs 2025 report (DESNZ, January 2026), pages 29 onwards. The figures shown are Levelised Cost of Electricity (LCOE) estimates for projects commissioning in 2030 — the average lifetime cost of building and operating a power plant per MWh generated. As such, they are estimates and in some cases have been simplified.

DESNZ Generation Costs 2025

Gas: Only the highest-efficiency CCGT operating at 93% load factor is shown. In practice, gas plants often run at much lower load factors (30–40%), which significantly increases the cost per MWh. The actual cost of gas generation is likely higher than this estimate.

Wind: Wind generation costs vary widely depending on whether turbines are onshore (~£58/MWh), fixed offshore (~£105/MWh), or floating offshore (~£155/MWh). The figure displayed is a capacity-weighted average of all three, based on UK installed capacity data from 2024: onshore 15.7 GW (50%), offshore 14.7 GW (47%), floating 0.78 GW (3%).
